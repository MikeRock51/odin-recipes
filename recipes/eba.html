Eba Recipe


Description

Eba is a type of food staple (swallow) popularly eaten in Nigeria and by Nigerians all over the world.

It is made with fried cassava called garri and hot boiling water to form a smooth stiff dough.

Garri comes in two forms, yellow garri which contains palm oil or white garri which is fried without palm oil.

It is best eaten with Nigerian soups such as Okra, Afang soup, Efo riro, Egusi soup and many more.

Ingredients

  • Boiled Water
  • Garri (dried, grated cassava (manioc) flour)
  • A little bit of energy

Steps

  1. First, heat water on the fire and allow boiling.
  2. Add boiling water in a bowl, then add garri
  3. Turn with turning spoon or spatula till comes together and smooth
  4. Serve and enjoy with soup of choice
